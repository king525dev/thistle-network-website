# Scottish Apprenticeship Network (SAN)

## 1. Mission Statement

To build a vibrant, community-led network for current and aspiring Scottish apprentices, bridging the gap between school, the workplace, and the social experience often lost by not going to university.



## 2. Vision

A Scotland where every apprentice — aspiring and current — regardless of background, has access to opportunities, connections, and confidence to thrive both professionally and personally.



## 3. Core Objectives

### Support Aspiring Apprentices

- Help young people through the apprenticeship application process with workshops, mentoring, and real-world insight days.
- Partner with top Scottish employers (e.g. Accenture, EY, Barclays, BT, ScottishPower, etc.) to host **Insight Days focused on apprenticeship pathways**, not graduate schemes.

### Empower Current Apprentices

- Build WhatsApp groups segmented by industry and location to connect current apprentices.
- Provide a space for friendship, advice, and peer support — replicating the “social experience” of university life.

### Create Social & Development Opportunities

- Organise trips, events, and potentially retreats across Scotland.
- Build a strong community, support network, and social group where young apprentices can meet and connect.

### Promote Financial Literacy

- Run sessions on budgeting, saving, investing, and long-term wealth building tailored for apprentices who start earning early.

### Build Organisational Partnerships

- Collaborate with leading Scottish employers, Skills Development Scotland, and apprenticeship providers.
- Partner with **Black Professionals UK (BPUK)** to connect SAN with **Black Apprentices Scotland (BAS)**.
- Work with **Big Career Conference** to host a SAN stall annually — helping apprentices sign up, connect, and discover opportunities.



## 4. Target Audience

### Aspiring Apprentices (16–20)

- Students in school or college looking to apply for apprenticeships.

### Current Apprentices (16–25)

- Apprentices across Scotland seeking support, friendships, and personal development.

### Corporate Partners

- Employers looking to improve early talent engagement and diversify apprenticeship pipelines.



## 5. Core Activities

| Category      | Key Actions                                                                                     |
| ------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Career Access | Run apprenticeship-focused insight days, company tours, and online Q&As                         |
| Community     | Launch and manage WhatsApp group chats (regional and by industry)                               |
| Events        | Organise career meetups, retreats, and social mixers                                            |
| Partnerships  | Collaborate with corporate sponsors and organisations                                           |
| Visibility    | Maintain presence at career fairs, including annual SAN activation at the Big Career Conference |
| Education     | Deliver finance and personal development sessions (budgeting, mindset, leadership)              |



## 6. Organisational Structure

### Founders / Director

- Oversees strategy, partnerships, and overall vision  
- **ZLD & Ashraf**

### Core Committee

A small leadership team responsible for:

- Events & Logistics
- Partnerships & Outreach
- Communications & Social Media
- Apprentice Relations (WhatsApp community management)

### Mentor Pool

- Experienced apprentices or professionals offering 1:1 or group guidance.



## 7. Partnership Model

| Partner Type       | Value to SAN                                 | Value to Partner                                 |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Corporates         | Insight days, sponsorship of retreats/events | Access to pre-qualified, motivated early talent  |
| BPUK / BAS         | Connection with Black apprentices nationwide | Joint visibility and shared pipeline             |
| Career Conferences | SAN stall and speaker slots                  | Increased attendance, credibility, and diversity |
| Colleges / Schools | Access to aspiring apprentices               | Clear progression pathways for students          |



## 8. Marketing & Growth Strategy

- **Digital Community:** Central WhatsApp hub supported by LinkedIn and Instagram presence.
- **School & College Outreach:** Visits and collaborations with careers departments.
- **Annual Anchor Event:** Strong SAN presence at the Big Career Conference.
- **Word-of-Mouth Growth:** Encourage members to bring a friend — *“each one, reach one”*.



## 9. Funding & Sustainability

### Initial Costs

- Event logistics (venue hire, refreshments, materials)
- Marketing and media (graphics, social content, videography)
- Retreats and trips (lodging and transport)

### Funding Sources

- Corporate sponsorships
- Event partnerships
- Grants from the Scottish Government
- Paid workshops or ticketed retreats
